We published a comprehensive review in *Clinical Chemistry* summarizing the role of extracellular vesicles (EVs) as liquid biopsy biomarkers across all stages of cancer — from early detection to recurrence monitoring.

The review covers EV biomarker performance for cancer screening, diagnosis, prognosis, treatment response prediction, and minimal residual disease monitoring. We highlight key clinical trial data, discuss biological and technological challenges in the EV biomarker field, and propose a framework for navigating the biomarker discovery pipeline.

A key theme is the emerging potential of ultrasensitive single-EV detection platforms, which may enable cancer detection at tumor sizes well below the current limits of clinical imaging — opening the door to truly early cancer interception.

We also introduce the "secondary EV release" hypothesis, proposing that tumor-derived EVs educate surrounding stromal cells to release a distinct population of tumor-educated EVs, expanding the pool of disease-relevant biomarkers in circulation.

**Citation:** Rayamajhi S, et al. *Clinical Chemistry.* 2024;70(1):206–219. https://doi.org/10.1093/clinchem/hvad176
