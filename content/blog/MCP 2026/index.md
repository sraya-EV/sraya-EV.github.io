Our new study published in *Molecular & Cellular Proteomics* identifies circulating extracellular vesicle (EV) protein biomarkers for early detection of high-grade serous ovarian cancer (HGSOC) — one of the deadliest gynecological cancers with a 5-year survival rate of less than 50%.

Using LC–MS/MS proteomics of plasma-derived small EVs from a 70-sample case–control cohort, we identified 1,078 EV-associated proteins, including 52 upregulated in early-stage HGSOC. Seven candidate biomarkers (MYL6, GSTP1, TTYH3, PRDX6, MUC1, MYH14, and PTGS1) were validated in fallopian tube tissue harboring precursor STIC lesions — the earliest detectable stage of ovarian cancer.

A four-protein combinatorial panel (MUC1, MYL6, TTYH3, and GSTP1) achieved an AUC of 0.975 with 90% sensitivity at 95% specificity for distinguishing early-stage HGSOC from healthy controls — a promising step toward a minimally invasive blood test for ovarian cancer screening.

**Citation:** Rayamajhi S, et al. *Mol Cell Proteomics.* 2026;25(2):101508. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mcpro.2026.101508
